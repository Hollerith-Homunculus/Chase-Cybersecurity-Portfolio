# GDPR Compliance Checklist

**Document ID**: CCF-CHK-002  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Effective Date**: May 2026  
**Owner**: Chase Cooley, CoolUnderFire Cybersecurity LLC  
**Purpose**: Quick reference checklist for basic GDPR readiness in the home lab and client support engagements (focused on EU data subjects or relevant processing).

## 1. Lawfulness, Fairness & Transparency
- [ ] Lawful basis for processing documented (consent, contract, legitimate interest, etc.)
- [ ] Privacy notice provided to data subjects
- [ ] Processing activities recorded (Article 30)

## 2. Data Minimization & Purpose Limitation
- [ ] Only collect data that is necessary
- [ ] Data used only for specified, legitimate purposes
- [ ] Data retention policy in place (delete when no longer needed)

## 3. Accuracy & Storage Limitation
- [ ] Processes to keep data accurate and up to date
- [ ] Retention periods defined and enforced
- [ ] Secure deletion methods used

## 4. Integrity & Confidentiality (Security)
- [ ] Appropriate technical measures (encryption, access controls, Wazuh monitoring)
- [ ] Least privilege applied
- [ ] Regular security testing and patching
- [ ] Breach detection and response processes (Wazuh + IR playbook)

## 5. Accountability & Governance
- [ ] Records of processing activities maintained
- [ ] Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) for high-risk processing
- [ ] Data processor agreements in place (if applicable)
- [ ] Staff training on data protection basics

## 6. Data Subject Rights
- [ ] Processes for handling requests (access, rectification, erasure, objection)
- [ ] Right to be forgotten procedure documented
- [ ] Response time target: 1 month (extendable)

## 7. Breach Notification
- [ ] Procedure to detect and respond to personal data breaches
- [ ] Notification to supervisory authority within 72 hours (if required)
- [ ] Communication to data subjects when high risk

## 8. International Transfers (if applicable)
- [ ] Safeguards in place for transfers outside EEA (Standard Contractual Clauses, etc.)

## Review & Maintenance
- [ ] Checklist reviewed annually or after significant changes
- [ ] Policies updated as needed
- [ ] Documentation retained for audit purposes

## Notes for CoolUnderFire / Lab Use
- This is a simplified checklist for small-scale operations. Full GDPR compliance requires legal advice for complex processing.
- Focus on technical controls (access control, encryption, logging, incident response) that we can demonstrate in the lab.
